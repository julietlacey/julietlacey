Written for Nimbus Literary Arts Magazine (Fall Edition, 2019)

Of Many Sparkling Obituaries

The benefit of a slow death is a

Measure in time

To grieve with the dying.

It’s a complicated sort of positive, but it is

This length of limbo that has let me write my father’s

Obituary again and again on sunny-headed nights.

One main snare is that the writing will have years of editing

To live up to—

Topics and symbols overturned, laid away, picked back up again.

But any piece of writing, as I’m still figuring out, can only ever be

A reflection of the writer at the time she writes

(Disappointing as this may be, given our endless critique of our past selves).

I’ll have time to write for the audience a summation of one life,

An ode to children and fathers; sea after storm.

Elbows soft on podium edge I’ll have called for Didion,

Or the more-dead, for guidance.

When I am seventy, and a

Far better writer because of it—

Even early as his ‘too-young’ age,

Maybe

My mind will persist

And the writing that is my self-reflection

Will reflect something of him too.

My Friend

When the screen blinks white I don’t always remember

That art is the imitation

Of true substance,

Ancient and abiding.

I pity those who close their eyes to it;

Those that razor sharp lines through the world,

Wrap themselves in cloaks of thick velvet.

But we! We have hidden beneath the drapes of staggering lives

And we have known what it is

To be tethered.

Few have felt it so pure.
